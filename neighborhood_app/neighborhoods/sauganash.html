Neighborhood Description

Potawatomi Chief Billy Caldwell "spent a good part of his life" negotiating treaties with the United States on behalf of the United Nations of the Chippewa, Ottawa and Potawotomi, and for his peacekeeping work, the United States government awarded him 1600 acres on the Chicago River, a plot of land that is now home to the Sauganash, Forest Glen and Edgebrook neighborhoods. Caldwell, who was was of mixed Irish and Native American ancestry, was referred to as Sauganash ("the Englishman") by Native Americans.
